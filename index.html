Step 6 of 7 -- Performance Verdict
Foundation Performance Opinion
Functional Utility
How has foundation movement affected the structure's day-to-day function?
Full Functional Utility
All doors, windows, and systems function normally. No floor slope perceptible to occupants.
Minimally Diminished Utility
Minor cosmetic evidence present but no operational impact.
Diminished Functional Utility
One or more doors or windows sticking or requiring force. Floor slope perceptible underfoot.
Significantly Impaired Utility
Multiple doors or windows non-functional. Visible floor slope. Daily function compromised.
Detectability Without Equipment
Was movement or unlevelness noticeable to the naked eye or perceptible underfoot without specialized measuring equipment?
Not detectable without equipment
Movement would not have been apparent without specialized elevation measurement tools
Observable without equipment
Movement or slope was perceptible to the naked eye or underfoot
Where was the unlevelness observable?
Main living areas (ground floor)
Primarily or exclusively upper level
Both levels
Framing Consideration: Upper-level-only movement warrants consideration of framing as a contributing or alternative cause. This distinction will be addressed in the performance opinion.
The verdict reflects the full picture -- elevation data, functional utility, detectability, observed distress, and professional judgment. Independent of elevation classification.
